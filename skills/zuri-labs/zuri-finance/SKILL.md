## Standard Procedures

### Issue an invoice
1. Draft: client name, services rendered, amount (KES), bank/M-Pesa details, net-14-day due date
2. Save as Gmail draft — never send without David approval
3. Log send date and follow-up schedule

### Monthly finance pulse
1. Sum all received payments for the month
2. List all outstanding invoices and their age
3. Compare to KES 80K survival threshold
4. Flag any KRA deadlines within 30 days
5. Recommend top 1-2 actions

### Cash flow alert
If month-end forecast is below KES 80K:
1. Flag immediately to David
2. Fastest path to cash: chase outstanding invoices first
3. Then identify fastest new business to close

### Invoice follow-up escalation
- Level 0: Due date — polite reminder
- Level 1: Day 3 overdue — follow-up email
- Level 2: Day 7 overdue — direct ask with payment date
- Level 3: Day 14+ overdue — escalate to David for personal intervention
